the backbone · nobody published this map before

Who actually carries ETF liquidity

Every ETF must report, once a year, which firms did its create and redeem work and for how many dollars. We collected those filings across 160 trusts, resolved every name to its parent, and mapped 4.64 trillion dollars of primary market flow. The result is the first public picture of how few shoulders the ETF market stands on.

30.6%
of all mapped flow runs through one banking group, Bank of America, across 831 funds
49.9%
the median fund routes half its primary market flow through a single parent firm
3 firms
carry 99.9% of the flow behind BND, a thirty billion dollar a year bond ETF held in millions of retirement accounts
700 funds
depend on one trading firm, Jane Street, for breadth. Banks carry the depth, one firm carries the long tail
Why it matters: when a single provider steps back, this map says exactly which funds feel it first. India saw the live version in July 2025 when one firm was barred and options volumes visibly sagged. We can now draw that scenario, fund by fund, for the US market.
the method · borrowed from the failure radar

Part of the LiquiLens engine, pointed at a new field

  • Public data only. SEC filings, NY Fed dealer statistics, Treasury auction results, FRED, live order books. If a reading cannot be reproduced from free sources, it does not ship.
  • Point in time, always. Every reading carries the date the data described, the date it was publicly knowable, and the date we published. Historical joins are honest by construction.
  • A tamper evident record. Publications are immutable, corrections are new dated revisions with stated reasons, and every release is committed to a hash chain. Ask any vendor for the hash of what they told you last quarter.
  • Published misses. Rules are registered before fitting. Failed studies are published, not retuned. This is the same practice behind the failure radar's validation record.
  • Screens, not advice. Undertow tells you where to look and how fragile the ground is. It does not tell you what to trade.
